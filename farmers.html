Grow with Jufra

Fair prices, consistent demand, and a direct market for your indigenous grain harvest.

Why Partner with Jufra

A fair, direct market for your grain

Jufra buys sorghum, finger millet, pearl millet and grain amaranth directly from smallholder farmers across Tharaka-Nithi County and beyond — at consistent, fair farmgate prices.

Becoming a Jufra supplier means a predictable market for your harvest, technical support to improve yield and quality, and a role in a supply chain that turns your grain into nutrition for families across Kenya.

Grain Buying Programme

How the programme works

1. Register

Submit the farmer registration form below with your location and grain type.

2. Assessment

Our field team visits to assess your farm and grain quality potential.

3. Agreement

We agree on volumes, pricing and a delivery schedule ahead of harvest.

4. Delivery & Payment

Deliver your harvest to an agreed collection point and get paid promptly.

Quality Standards

What we look for in grain

M

Moisture Content

Grain must be properly dried to Jufra's accepted moisture threshold.

P

Purity

Free from foreign matter, pests, and visible mold or damage.

V

Variety

Accepted indigenous varieties of sorghum, millet and amaranth.

S

Storage

Properly stored and bagged grain, ready for collection or delivery.

Extension Services

Field support that grows with you

Jufra's field officers provide agronomic guidance, harvest-timing advice, and post-harvest handling support to help farmers improve both yield and grain quality season over season.

Training Opportunities

Learning alongside the harvest

Periodic training sessions cover topics from soil health and drought-smart planting to proper drying and storage — helping farmer groups strengthen their long-term productivity.

FAQ

Common questions from farmers

How do I get paid?

Payments are made promptly after grain is received, weighed and quality-checked at the collection point.

What if my grain doesn't meet the moisture standard?

Our field team can advise on drying techniques ahead of your next delivery date.

Do I need a minimum volume to register?

No — both individual farmers and farmer groups or cooperatives are welcome to register.
