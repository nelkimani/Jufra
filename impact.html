Community Impact

Nutrition, dignity and shared prosperity

Every product sold funds fairer farmgate prices, farmer training and community nutrition programs.

Food Security

Consistent demand for indigenous grains gives farming households a more reliable source of income and food.

Nutrition

Naturally processed, additive-free flours bring calcium, iron and protein-rich staples back to the family table.

Farmer Empowerment

Fair, consistent pricing helps smallholder farmers plan their season with confidence rather than guesswork.

Women's Empowerment

Women make up a significant share of Jufra's supply chain, from farming to processing to distribution.

Youth Employment

Processing, packaging, logistics and sales roles created around Jufra's growth give young people local career paths.

Local Economic Development

Value addition keeps more of the grain's worth inside Tharaka-Nithi County, rather than exporting it as raw produce.

Recognized for Impact

Recognized for Creating Sustainable Impact

Jufra Food Processors was profiled by the Micro and Small Enterprises Authority (MSEA) as part of the World Bank-supported Kenya Jobs and Economic Transformation (KJET) Project — a national initiative highlighting Kenyan enterprises driving jobs through agro-processing and value addition.

The profiling recognized Jufra's work turning raw indigenous grains into market-ready, nutritious products, its strong farmer partnerships, and its role in creating more than 400 youth employment opportunities through seed development, aggregation, processing and distribution.

Sustainability

Growth that doesn't cost the future

Climate-Smart Agriculture

Indigenous grains like sorghum and millet are naturally drought-resilient, needing less water than maize.

Indigenous Grain Promotion

Reviving demand for traditional grains helps preserve crop diversity suited to Kenya's changing climate.

Sustainable Sourcing

Direct relationships with smallholder farmers mean shorter, more transparent supply chains.

Reduced Food Waste

Quality-tested processing reduces post-harvest losses that would otherwise go to waste.
