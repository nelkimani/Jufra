About Jufra

Rooted in Chuka. Grown for a continent.

The story of how a Tharaka-Nithi grain processor became a champion for indigenous nutrition across East Africa.

Our Story

Why Jufra was founded

Jufra Food Processors began operations in 2013 with a simple conviction: the grains our grandparents relied on — sorghum, finger millet, pearl millet and grain amaranth — are among the most nutritious foods on earth, and they had been pushed to the margins for too long.

Based in Chuka, Tharaka-Nithi County, Jufra works directly with smallholder farmers to turn these indigenous grains into natural, chemical-free, high-quality food products, while restoring dignity and income to the farmers who grow them.

Today, Jufra produces a family of porridge, composite and pure grain flours trusted by households, retailers and institutions across Kenya.

Mission & Vision

What drives us every day

Our Mission

To create wealth and food security for smallholder dryland farmers, and to bring consumers safe, high-quality natural foods that provide optimal nutrition.

Our Vision

To be a premier company in the processing and marketing of dryland grains — recognized across East Africa for nutrition, quality and farmer partnership.

Leadership

The team behind Jufra

Four directors guiding Jufra's mission to bring indigenous grain nutrition to families across East Africa.

Julius Mwebia

Director

Julius Mwebia is a Director of Jufra Food Processors and a driving force behind the company's mission to restore indigenous grains to the center of Kenyan diets. He holds a Bachelor's degree in Agribusiness and is currently pursuing a Master's degree in Environmental Science at Chuka University. Before co-founding Jufra, he served as an Agricultural Extension Officer with Kenya's Ministry of Agriculture. In 2013, he left public service to devote himself fully to building Jufra Agro Strategies and Jufra Food Processors.

  • Agribusiness development
  • Farmer engagement & extension
  • Sustainable agriculture
  • Indigenous grain value addition
  • Rural economic development

Frashiah Wangeci

Director

Frashiah Wangeci brings a strong foundation in environmental and agricultural science to Jufra's leadership. She holds a Master's degree in Environmental Studies and a Bachelor's degree in Agribusiness. Prior to joining Jufra, she served as an officer with Kenya's Ministry of Agriculture, working closely with farming communities. Evelyne Wanja
